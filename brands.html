Lenovo

  1. Lenovo is the world's largest personal computer vendor by unit sales as of 2021. Official known as Lenovo Group Limited, it is a Chinese multinational technology company that manufacturesdesktop computers, laptops, tablet computers, smartphones, workstations, servers, supercomputers, electronic storage devices, IT management software, and smart televisions. Its best-known brand in the West is IBM's ThinkPad business line of laptop computers.
  2. It also makes the IdeaPad, Yoga, and Legion consumer lines of laptop computers, and the IdeaCentre and ThinkCentre lines of desktop computers.
  3. In 2022, Lenovo’s revenues US$71.6 billion, with an operating income of US$3.1 billion and a net income of US$2.1 billion. Its total assets in 2022 were US$44.51 billion and its total equity was US$5.395 billion.
  4. That year the company had 75.000 employees.

Canon

  1. Canon is one of the world’s leading manufacturers of copiers, printers, cameras, and equipment used to make semiconductors and liquid crystal displays (LCD).
  2. Its has three major divisions: business machines, cameras, and optical and other products.
  3. Canon was the world’s largest digital camera maker in 2007, holding a 17.3 percent share in the compact digital camera market in unit terms, compared with second-ranked Sony at 16.5 percent, according to research firm IDC.
  4. In SLR models, Canon controlled 42.7 percent of the global market, while Nikon held 40 percent.
  5. Canon was the world's second-largest maker of copiers in 2007 with 20.5 percent of the market in value terms, according to research firm Gartner.

Microsoft

  1. Founded on April 4, 1975, by Bill Gates and Paul Allen, Microsoft is one of the largest and most successful companies in the world.
  2. Microsoft is the developer and distributor of Microsoft Windows, Microsoft Office, DirectX, Xbox, and numerous other programs, games, and services.
  3. Microsoft’s corporate mission is “to empower every person and every organization on the planet to achieve more.” The following components are significant in Microsoft’s corporate mission statement:
  4. Empowerment,Every person and every organization on the planet, To achieve more.
  5. Microsoft’s corporate vision is “to help people and businesses throughout the world realize their full potential.”

Samsung

  1. Samsung, South Korean company that is one of the world’s largest producers of electronic devices.
  2. Samsung specializes in the production of a wide variety of consumer and industry electronics, including appliances, digital media devices, semiconductors, memory chips, and integrated systems.
  3. It has become one of the most-recognizable names in technology and produces about a fifth of South Korea’s total exports.
  4. Samsung Electronics Co., Ltd. engages in the manufacturing and selling of electronics and computer peripherals.
  5. The company operates through following business divisions: Consumer Electronics, Information Technology & Mobile Communications and Device Solutions.
  6. The Information Technology & Mobile Communications business division offers handheld products, communication systems, computers and digital cameras.
  7. The company was founded on January 13, 1969 and is headquartered in Suwon, South Korea.

Apple

  1. Apple is one of the leading branding companies in the world. Marketing experts like Marc Gobe argue that Apple's brand is the key to the company's success.
  2. It's got nothing to do with products like the iMac or iPod. View Slideshow View Slideshow Ask marketers and advertising experts why Mac users are so loyal, and they all cite the same reason: Apple's brand.
  3. "People talk about technology, but Apple was a marketing company," Sculley told the Guardian newspaper in 1997. "It was the marketing company of the decade."
  4. The current CEO, Steve Jobs, spent $100 million marketing the iMac, which was a run-away hit.
  5. Apple continues to spend lots of money on high-profile ads like the "Switch" campaign, and it shows.
  6. "It's a really powerful brand," said Robin Rusch, editor of the Brandchannel.com, which awarded Apple "Brand of the Year" in 2001.
